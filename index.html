🌍 Invasive Plants & Vegetables Defender

Learn the rules before you play!

🎯

How to Play

Choose a continent and you'll be taken to a 5×5 grid filled with plants. Your job is to identify and remove invasive plants while keeping the native plants alive. Use the tools at the bottom of the screen to remove plants from each cell. Clear all invasive plants to complete the continent!

🌿

Native vs Invasive

Each cell contains either a native plant 🟢 or an invasive plant 🔴 — or it may be empty. Removing an invasive plant earns you +10 points. But if you accidentally remove a native plant, you lose a ❤️ life and must answer a quiz question. Lose all 5 lives and you'll need to answer 5 questions to restore them!

🧰

Your Tools

Select a tool from the toolbar at the bottom, then click a cell to use it. All tools remove whatever plant is in the cell — so choose carefully! You have five tools: 🌿 Spray, ⛏️ Hoe, 🔥 Flame, 🕸️ Net, and 💧 Water. The selected tool glows gold.

📖 Plant & Tool Guide

Know your plants — and your tools!

🧰 What Each Tool Does

🌿
Spray

Chemical herbicide spray. Fast and effective at killing invasive plants on contact. Use carefully — it kills natives too.

⛏️
Hoe

Digs out roots from the soil. Best for plants that spread underground. Removes the whole root system to stop regrowth.

🔥
Flame

Burns plants at the surface. Useful for dense invasive growth. Some native seeds need fire to germinate — use wisely!

🕸️
Net

Traps and removes plants without chemicals. Gentler method — good for areas near native plants you want to protect.

💧
Water

Floods the cell to drown shallow-rooted invasives. Also helps native plants recover after nearby invasives are removed.

❓ Q&A

What is a native plant?
A native plant is a plant that naturally grows in a certain area without being brought there by people.
What is an invasive plant?
An invasive plant is a plant that has been introduced to an area and spreads quickly, often causing harm.
How can you tell if a plant is native or invasive?
You can check whether the plant originally came from that area and whether it is growing naturally or taking over other plants.
Why can invasive plants be a problem?
They can spread fast, crowd out native plants, and change habitats.
Why are native plants important?
They provide food and shelter for local animals and help keep ecosystems balanced.
Can a plant be native in one country but invasive in another?
Yes. A plant may be native in one place but invasive when introduced somewhere else.
What happens when invasive plants take over an area?
They can reduce biodiversity by pushing out other plant species.
How do invasive plants usually spread to new places?
They can spread by people, animals, wind, water, or vehicles.
What is biodiversity?
Biodiversity means having many different kinds of living things in one area.
Why do native animals often depend on native plants?
Native animals may use native plants for food, shelter, and breeding places.
What is one sign that a plant might be invasive?
It spreads very quickly and grows over large areas fast.
Are all non-native plants invasive?
No. Some non-native plants do not cause harm and are not invasive.
Why might humans plant species from other continents?
For gardens, food, farming, or decoration.
What is one way to protect native plants?
Do not plant invasive species and remove them when needed.
Why is it important to learn about invasive and native plants around the world?
It helps us protect ecosystems and understand how plants affect environments in different continents.
